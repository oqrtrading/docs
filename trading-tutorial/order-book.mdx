---
title: "1. The order book"
description: "What you see when you open a market: bids, asks, and levels."
---

When you open a market, you see an **order book**. It’s the list of buy and sell orders at each price.

- **Bids** are buy orders. The **best bid** is the highest price someone is willing to pay.
- **Asks** are sell orders. The **best ask** is the lowest price someone is willing to sell at.
- The **spread** is the gap between best bid and best ask. A tight spread usually means more liquidity.
- Each **level** is a price and a size: e.g. “55¢ bid for 100 contracts.” The book is just levels on both sides.

So when we say “the book,” we mean: at every price, how much is bid and how much is offered. When a bid and an ask cross, the venue matches a trade. Your job is to decide where you want to buy or sell relative to that book.
