---
title: "2. Position"
description: "Your net exposure: long, short, and one number for risk."
---

Your **position** in a contract is your net exposure: how many you own (long) or have sold (short).

- **Long** means you own contracts. You profit if the contract pays out (e.g. yes settles at 100¢).
- **Short** means you’ve sold contracts you didn’t own—or you bought the opposite side (e.g. no). You profit if the contract doesn’t pay.
- We write position as one number: positive = long, negative = short.

In **binary** markets there are two sides: **yes** (pays 100¢ if the event happens) and **no** (pays 100¢ if it doesn’t). Yes + no = 100¢. You can trade either side; we often express everything in one side (e.g. “yes-equivalent”) so we have one number for risk.
