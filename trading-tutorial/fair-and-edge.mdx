---
title: "3. Fair and edge"
description: "Your valuation and your reason to trade."
---

Before you trade, you need a **fair** and an **edge**.

- **Fair** is the price you’re willing to buy or sell around—your valuation. It’s the number you give the system so it knows where you think value is. You want to buy below fair and sell above it.
- **Edge** is the reason you trade: you think you have an advantage. If your fair is 60¢ and the market is 55¢, you have edge buying at 55¢. No edge means you’re just crossing the spread for no expected gain.

So: fair is your level; edge is why you’re willing to trade at that level. We go deeper in [Fair values](/trading/fair-values).
