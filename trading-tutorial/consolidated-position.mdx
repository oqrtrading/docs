---
title: "6. Your consolidated position"
description: "One number for your net exposure per group."
---

When you have positions on multiple legs in the same group, we combine them into **one** number: your **consolidated position**.

- We express every leg in the reference unit (e.g. yes). Long yes adds to the consolidated position; long no (flipped) subtracts.
- Example: long 30 yes → +30, long 70 no → −70 in yes-terms. **Consolidated position = +30 − 70 = −40** (you’re net short 40 in yes-equivalent).

That number answers: “What’s my net exposure in one unit?” It’s the number you use for risk, sizing, and P&amp;L. In the Terminal you’ll see **Consolidated** (position per group) and **By ticker** (raw position per symbol). For thinking about risk and size, use consolidated.
