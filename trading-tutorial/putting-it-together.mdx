---
title: "8. Putting it together"
description: "Recap and how to use this when you trade."
---

You’ve now seen the chain:

1. **Order book** — Bids and asks at each level; best bid and best ask.
2. **Position** — Your net exposure (long/short) in a contract; in binaries, yes and no.
3. **Fair and edge** — Your level (fair) and your reason to trade (edge).
4. **Consolidated orderbook** — One book from multiple legs, with orientation, so you see one BBO in one unit.
5. **Consolidated position** — One number for your net exposure per group, in the same unit as the book and your consolidated orders.
6. **Why it matters** — Risk, P&amp;L, sizing, collateral return (e.g. on Kalshi), and a single mental model for the Terminal or API.

**When you trade:**

- **Watch your consolidated position** — In the blotter, track consolidated position per group so you know your net exposure and when you’re flat (and when you get collateral back).
- **Trade in one unit** — Use the consolidated book and place consolidated orders so you think “buy 50 at 55¢” instead of splitting legs yourself.
- **Use fair and edge** — Set fair where you think value is; only trade when you have edge.
